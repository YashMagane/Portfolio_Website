My Adventures in Computer Science

Exploring Deep Learning, Neural Networks, and CUDA Programming

April 2, 2025

DeepSeek and Neural Networks

Lately, I've been diving deep into some fascinating areas of AI and computing, and I wanted to share what I’ve been learning! From neural networks to GPU computing, it's been an exciting journey, and I feel like I’m only scratching the surface.


One of the key topics I explored was DeepSeek, an AI research project focused on deep learning. Neural networks have always intrigued me, but learning more about reinforcement learning, matrix multiplication, and vectors has given me a clearer picture of how they actually function. Understanding these mathematical foundations has helped me appreciate how models learn and optimize their performance over time.


Reinforcement Learning: Teaching AI to Make Decisions

Reinforcement learning (RL) is another concept that caught my attention. Unlike traditional supervised learning, where models learn from labeled data, RL involves training an agent to make decisions by interacting with an environment. The AI receives rewards or penalties based on its actions, gradually improving its decision-making abilities. This is the kind of technique used in game-playing AIs and robotics.


The World of GPU Computing and CUDA Programming

Another huge revelation for me was the importance of GPU computing in AI. Unlike traditional CPUs, GPUs handle computations in parallel, making them ideal for deep learning tasks. I also learned about CUDA, a parallel computing platform by NVIDIA that allows developers to write programs that run efficiently on GPUs. Writing in CUDA opens up new possibilities for optimizing AI workloads and accelerating complex computations.


Shoutout to Computerphile!

A big thanks to the YouTube channel Computerphile for making these advanced topics easier to understand! Their videos break down complex concepts in a way that’s accessible and engaging. If you’re curious about AI, computing, or just tech in general, I highly recommend checking them out.


I’m excited to continue this learning journey and explore more about deep learning, AI optimization, and parallel computing. If you have any recommendations or resources, feel free to share!

Dual Booting Linux

March 21, 2025

Facing My Linux Knowledge Gap: A Journey from Interview to Dual Booting...

Recently, I had an interview for an Observability Engineer role, and while I was excited about the opportunity, I quickly realized I had a massive gap in my knowledge—Linux. During the interview, I was asked about system monitoring, log aggregation, and troubleshooting, and it became painfully clear that my understanding of Linux was nowhere near where it needed to be. I had dabbled in Linux before, but I never truly understood its importance until that moment.


This realization made me take a step back. I had to go back to the basics, not just of Linux, but of operating systems in general. What makes an OS different from another? How does Linux compare to Windows, which I was so comfortable with? Unlike Windows, where everything is abstracted and simplified for the user, Linux exposes the raw mechanics of how an operating system interacts with hardware and software. Concepts like the filesystem hierarchy, user permissions, package management, and the terminal-driven nature of Linux started to make more sense to me.


Dual Booting Linux

As I researched more, I stumbled upon something interesting—dual booting. Instead of running Linux on a virtual machine, I learned that I could install it alongside Windows and switch between them at boot. This sounded like a perfect way to fully immerse myself in Linux without giving up my existing setup. But there was a catch: dual booting isn’t exactly beginner-friendly. The process involves manual partitioning, which, if done incorrectly, could wipe out all my data. That was terrifying. One wrong move, and everything—my projects, software, and personal files—could disappear in an instant.


This brought me to one of the most important lessons of this journey: back up everything before making major system changes. I made sure to back up all my critical files before proceeding. I then carefully followed the steps to create partitions, install Linux, and set up a bootloader. The experience was nerve-wracking, but once I successfully booted into Linux for the first time, I felt an incredible sense of accomplishment.


Using Linux

Now that I have a proper Linux setup, my goal is to use it as my learning environment to explore the fundamentals of servers, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and system administration. These are crucial skills for any engineer working in observability and infrastructure. By working with Linux daily, I can familiarize myself with terminal commands, scripting, system processes, and security configurations—all of which will be invaluable in my career.


This entire experience has been a humbling reminder that learning is never linear. Sometimes, you have to take a step back to move forward. If you're considering dual booting, my advice is to research thoroughly, prepare for the worst, and always have a backup. It’s scary, but the rewards are well worth it.


This is just the beginning of my Linux journey, but I’m already seeing how it’s shaping my understanding of the tech world. If you’ve been hesitant about learning Linux, I’d say go for it—you’ll gain insights that go far beyond just another operating system.

Week 1 Bootcamp

Sept 18, 2025

Plain Ol' HTML - Mastering the fundamentals and collaboration...

Week one of the bootcamp has been an eye-opening introduction to what working in tech really feels like. To mirror real-life work environments, we’ve been doing daily standups and retrospectives. These short, structured meetings helped us build accountability, reflect on progress, and adapt quickly— very much in line with the Agile process we’ll be using for our upcoming projects.


Collaboration was a huge theme this week. I was introduced to multiple ways of working together: using Live Share in VS Code for real-time coding, practicing driver and navigator roles for pair programming, and relying on Slack for calls alongside Google Meet for group meetings. Each tool had its own strengths, and together they created a smooth workflow that kept everyone aligned.


Another highlight was learning about success logs. This simple practice of recording achievements, no matter how small, really boosted motivation and gave me a better perspective on my own progress throughout the week.


Technical Progress

On the technical side, one of my biggest wins was mastering the fundamentals of semantic HTML tags. Instead of just throwing everything into <div> elements, I now understand the importance of using elements like <header>, <section>, <article>, and <footer>. This not only improves accessibility but also makes my code cleaner and easier to maintain.


Looking back, this first week has already given me a strong foundation in teamwork, communication, and technical skills. If this is just the beginning, I’m excited to see what the rest of the bootcamp will bring.

By the end of the week, seeing HappyToes come to life felt incredibly rewarding. It wasn’t just about building a website; it was about building trust, communication, and good development habits.
